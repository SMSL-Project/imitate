# Puzzle settings

The puzzle is the chess board task with two pieces: star chess and circle chess. The valid board positions are `A`, `B`, `C`, `D`, `a`, `c`, `d`, `0`, and `1`. The positions marked `X` in the board layout are impassable.

Board layout:
- First row: A, B, C, D
- Second row: a, X, c, d
- Third row: 0, X, X, 1

# Rule settings

- Pieces may move only between adjacent valid positions on the board.
- Pieces may stack on the same valid position.
- If both pieces occupy the same position, only the piece on top may move away from that stack.
- If a piece moves onto a position already occupied by the other piece, the moved piece becomes the top piece in the resulting state.

If both pieces occupy the same position, the state must encode which piece is on top.

Adjacency is only through direct orthogonal neighbors that are valid squares. Squares separated by an `X` cell are not adjacent. In particular, `a` is not adjacent to `c`, and `0` is not adjacent to `1`.

The complete adjacency graph is:
- A: [B, a]
- B: [A, C]
- C: [B, D, c]
- D: [C, d]
- a: [A, 0]
- c: [C, d]
- d: [D, c, 1]
- 0: [a]
- 1: [d]
